COVID-19 IN ENGLAND AND WALES

November, 2020 

This chapter illustrates my work on the geography of COVID-19 in England and Wales. Using data sourced from the Office for National Statistics and Our World in Data, I have generated a few maps on the locational variations in deaths from COVID-19 and also total cases.

My initial projection shows the overall cases of COVID-19 in the UK throughout 2000 and 2022. Made in RStudio using the code generated by Ansgar Wolsing, the graphic shows the seven-day average and it is weighted heavily in the time periods where the UK has experienced the highest numbers of cases.

The following maps use data from an earlier time period. I used QGIS to generate two cartograms which distort the data towards the higher totals, with the individual figure overlaid in choropleth form, and included a map of England and Wales to show the territories in their usual shape. The calculation for the mass-based cartogram measures the deaths by COVID-19 data over the whole surface of each polygon. This projects Greater London as a similar size to other regions.
